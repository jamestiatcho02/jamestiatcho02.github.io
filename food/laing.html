Laing

Laing is a Filipino dish of shredded or whole taro leaves with meat or seafood cooked in thick coconut milk spiced with labuyo chili, lemongrass, garlic, shallots, ginger, and shrimp paste. It originates from the Bicol Region, where it is known simply as pinangat.

Ingredients


1 pack dried taro leaves

1/2 lb. pork cut into thin strips

1/2 lb. shrimp with head (optional)

1/2 cup shrimp paste

1 medium onion chopped

6 pcs Thai chili

1/4 cup ginger cut into strips

1 tbsp garlic minced

3 cups Coconut milk

2 tbsp fish sauce

Salt and pepper to taste

Procedures


Procedure #1

In a large pan, pour-in the Coconut milk and bring to a boil


Procedure #2

Add the garlic, onion, and ginger. Simmer for 10 minute


Procedure #3

sAdd the pork meat and shrimp paste and stir continuously


Procedure #4

Put in the Thai chili and simmer for another 10 minutes


Procedure #5

Add the fish sauce and dried Taro leaves and simmer for 15 to 20 minutes


Procedure #6

Put the shrimps in and simmer for 5 minutes


Procedure #7

Serve hot. Enjoy
